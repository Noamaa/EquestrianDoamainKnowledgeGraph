Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

In this work, we present an ontology specifically designed for the equestrian research domain, developed in collaboration with the French Institute for Horse and Riding (IFCE). The aim of this ontology is to structure and semantically link heterogeneous datasets produced by various equestrian-related research projects, including physiological sensor data, experimental protocols, video metadata, and project documentation.

The ontology captures key domain concepts such as Horse, Rider, Sensor, and Experiment, and formalizes the relationships between them. It has been implemented using OWL and published with Widoco.

Introduction back to ToC

The growing use of sensors and digital monitoring in equine research has led to the production of increasingly large and diverse datasets. These datasets are often collected independently and stored in heterogeneous formats, making it difficult to aggregate or exploit them coherently. To address this challenge, we propose an ontology tailored to the equestrian research domain. The ontology is designed to model key entities such as horses, riders, experiments, sensors, and projects, and to formalize the semantic relationships that link them.
